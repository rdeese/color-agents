What's important?

Now we understand how the critters changed color. It happened because of three ways that color behaves in critters:

  1. Color passes from parent to child.
  2. Color of children is sometimes surprising.
  3. Color affects how long a critter will probably live.

This is really important! If these three facts are true for any part of any living thing, then that part will change in the same way that the critters changed color. It could be size or shape, strength or speed, anything at all!

Who chose the critters' color?

Was it the critters? Critters can't change their own color, choose who they mate with, or choose what color their children will be. Critters don't even know what color their environment is!

Was it you? Maybe you think so. If you do, reset the critters and play again. This time, try to change their color to something other than the environment's color. I don't think you'll succeed!

Changes are part of nature.

For the critters in the wild, color

  • is passed from parent to child.
  • isn't always predictable.
  • affects how long the critter will probably live.

Because these three things are true, the color of the critters changed naturally. First, unpredicable new colors of critter were born. Some of them had colors that fit better with the environment, and so you were less likely to hunt them. Then, all the critters that you didn't hunt passed on their colors to their children.
