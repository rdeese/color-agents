This is called natural selection. It's a process that causes slow changes to living things.

Natural selection will do this every time.

No matter what color the environment is, or what color the critters start out with, they will become the same color as the environment if you hunt them. See for yourself by starting again with different colors.

So, who chose the critters' color? Nobody.

It might seem strange, but it turns out that nobody chose the critters' color. Because of natural selection, their color changed on its own.

But here's the cool part: because we know how natural selection works, we can notice when it has happened, and what it has done. Even though we don't get to choose the critters' color, we can understand why it changed.
