Color change in three steps.

What happened to make the critters change color? It's a simple process with three parts. Each of these parts is something you've already seen, but together they did something unexpected!

Natural Selection.

The combination of mutation, selection, and inheritance is called natural selection. Natural selection happens every time all three things are present, and it can't happen without all three of them.

Natural selection always causes some kind of change. You saw it change the critters' color, so they would blend in better with the environment. But there are no rules about what kinds of things natural selection can change.

Selection in control.

Mutation and inheritance are just part of the way critters are. Selection was the missing piece, and you caused selection by hunting the critters. In natural selection, the selection part controls what changes, and how. You hunted differently based on color, so that's what changed.

But when you're not hunting critters, their color doesn't matter. No selection means no natural selection, and no color change.

So it was you who changed the critters' color!

But you didn't choose it—natural selection did.
