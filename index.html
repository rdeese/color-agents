How did the critters change color?

The critters changed color in a three stage process, that happened over and over while you were watching and hunting them.

1. Mutation

Some of the ??? critters' children were surprisingly ??? or ???.

2. Survival

The ??? environment made ??? critters hard to see. You hunted ??? and ??? critters, but ??? critters stayed alive.

3. Inheritance

The ??? critters that you didn't hunt passed that ??? color to their children.

Together, these three stages are called natural selection. Natural selection is a process that causes slow changes to living things.

Why did hunting make a difference?

All groups of critters have mutation and inheritance. But without a survival stage, natural selection can't happen!

By hunting critters, you made a surivival stage where color mattered for survival. Critters with a color like the environment stayed alive, and all other critters got hunted.

Will natural selection do this every time?

Yes! No matter what color the environment is, or what color the critters start out with, they will become the same color as the environment if you hunt them. See for yourself by starting again with different colors.
